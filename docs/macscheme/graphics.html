MacScheme Guide

Graphics

MacScheme embeds an indexed-colour framebuffer in the Graphics pane. You draw into buffers with Scheme procedures and present frames explicitly with gfx-flip.

Quick start

(gfx-screen 320 240 1)
(gfx-rect 24 16 208 128 17)
(gfx-text 32 32 "HELLO" 18)
(gfx-flip)
  • gfx-screen creates or reconfigures the embedded graphics surface.
  • gfx-reset restores the default palette and clears the current target.
  • gfx-flip presents the current back buffer.
  • gfx-vsync and gfx-wait help pace animation loops.

Menu shortcuts

  • The Graphics menu can clear the pane without rerunning your program.
  • Built-in classic presets include 256×160, 320×200, 320×240, 512×320, 640×400, and 640×480.
  • Wide presets include 720×480, 800×450, and 854×480.

Drawing model

  • Build a frame with drawing commands such as gfx-line, gfx-rect, gfx-circle, gfx-triangle, and gfx-text.
  • Use gfx-set-target to draw into one of the extra buffers when you need off-screen composition.
  • Call gfx-flip after updating the frame you want shown.
