MacScheme Guide

Graphics

MacScheme embeds an indexed-colour framebuffer in the Graphics pane. You draw into buffers with Scheme procedures and present frames explicitly with gfx-flip.

Quick start

(gfx-screen 320 240 1)
(gfx-rect 24 16 208 128 17)
(gfx-text 32 32 "HELLO" 18)
(gfx-flip)
  • gfx-screen creates or reconfigures the embedded graphics surface.
  • gfx-reset restores the default palette and clears the current target.
  • gfx-flip presents the current back buffer.
  • gfx-vsync and gfx-wait help pace animation loops.

Menu shortcuts

  • The Graphics menu can clear the pane without rerunning your program.
  • Built-in classic presets include 256×160, 320×200, 320×240, 512×320, 640×400, and 640×480.
  • Wide presets include 720×480, 800×450, and 854×480.

Drawing model

  • Build a frame with drawing commands such as gfx-line, gfx-rect, gfx-circle, gfx-triangle, and gfx-text.
  • Use gfx-set-target to draw into one of the extra buffers when you need off-screen composition.
  • Call gfx-flip after updating the frame you want shown.

Indexed colour and palettes

The framebuffer is palette-driven. Most drawing functions take a colour index, not direct RGB values. That means you can redraw a scene once, then change how it looks by editing palette entries instead of repainting every pixel.

Default palette range

MacScheme ships with a practical default palette in the higher slots, especially indexes 16 through 31. Those are the values most examples use for backgrounds, highlights, warm accents, and text.

(gfx-reset)
(gfx-pal 19 90 140 220)
(gfx-pal 20 180 220 255)
(gfx-pal 24 255 200 96)

(gfx-rect 0 0 320 240 19)
(gfx-circle 160 120 40 24 #t)
(gfx-flip)
  • gfx-pal rewrites a global palette entry with RGB values from 0 to 255.
  • gfx-reset restores the palette and clears the current target, which is useful before starting a new visual style.
  • Changing one palette slot updates every pixel that already uses that index.

Line palettes

You can override palette entries for individual scanlines. This is a lightweight way to create horizon shifts, water bands, sky ramps, or heat-haze style colour transitions without copying buffers around.

(gfx-reset)
(gfx-rect 0 0 320 240 19)

; Give colour 19 a darker top and brighter lower half.
(gfx-line-pal 40 19 40 60 90)
(gfx-line-pal 120 19 90 140 220)
(gfx-line-pal 180 19 140 180 255)

(gfx-flip)
  • gfx-line-pal changes one palette index on one line.
  • For larger bands, apply a loop across a range of rows.
  • This is ideal when the shape stays fixed but the colour treatment needs to vary vertically.

Gradients and animated palette effects

MacScheme also supports time-based palette programs. These effects run by rewriting palette entries over time, which is a classic retro technique for water shimmer, neon pulsing, warning lights, and sky cycling.

; Create a line gradient for one palette slot.
(gfx-pal-gradient 0 19 0 159 10 20 50 120 180 255)

; Cycle a palette range back and forth.
(gfx-pal-cycle 1 24 31 2 1)

; Fade one colour between two RGB endpoints.
(gfx-pal-fade 2 20 3 60 80 140 180 220 255)

; Pulse a highlight colour.
(gfx-pal-pulse 3 24 4 255 180 90 255 255 210)

; Strobe a colour on and off.
(gfx-pal-strobe 4 30 8 8 255 255 255 40 40 60)
  • gfx-pal-gradient interpolates one palette entry over a line range.
  • gfx-pal-cycle and gfx-pal-cycle-lines rotate palette entries for flowing effects.
  • gfx-pal-fade and gfx-pal-fade-lines move one entry between two colours.
  • gfx-pal-pulse and gfx-pal-strobe are good for highlights, hazard lights, and UI emphasis.
  • Use gfx-pal-stop, gfx-pal-stop-all, gfx-pal-pause, and gfx-pal-resume to manage running effects.

Practical palette workflow

  • Pick a small set of colour indexes for a scene, then tune those entries with gfx-pal until the look feels right.
  • Use line palettes or gradients to avoid repainting large sky or background areas every frame.
  • Use palette cycling and pulsing when the artwork is static but you want motion in lava, stars, console lights, or energy bars.
  • Keep effect slots organized by role, for example slot 0 for gradients, slot 1 for water, slot 2 for warning flashes.

Small scene demos

These are compact palette-first recipes you can paste into the REPL. They assume a 320×240 screen and use palette changes for most of the look.

Sky

(gfx-screen 320 240 1)
(gfx-reset)
(gfx-rect 0 0 320 240 19)
(gfx-pal-gradient 0 19 0 159 16 28 72 120 190 255)
(gfx-pal-gradient 1 20 160 239 255 180 96 255 110 72)
(gfx-circle 250 56 18 20 #t)
(gfx-rect 0 160 320 80 17)
(gfx-flip)

A single background colour becomes a sky ramp, and a second palette slot gives the sun and horizon a warmer band.

Water

(gfx-screen 320 240 1)
(gfx-reset)
(gfx-rect 0 0 320 240 17)
(gfx-rect 0 110 320 130 19)
(gfx-pal-gradient 0 19 110 239 10 30 70 60 170 255)
(gfx-pal-cycle-lines 1 19 130 239 2 1)
(gfx-cycle #t)
(gfx-flip)

This keeps the geometry static and puts the motion into the lower scanlines by cycling the water colour treatment over time.

Lava

(gfx-screen 320 240 1)
(gfx-reset)
(gfx-rect 0 0 320 240 18)
(gfx-rect 0 150 320 90 24)
(gfx-pal 24 255 120 20)
(gfx-pal 25 255 200 40)
(gfx-pal 26 255 255 180)
(gfx-pal-cycle 2 24 26 2 1)
(gfx-pal-pulse 3 26 3 255 220 120 255 255 210)
(gfx-cycle #t)
(gfx-flip)

Three hot palette entries and a pulse on the brightest one are enough to get an active molten strip without repainting the whole surface.

Sprite effects

Sprites sit on top of the indexed-colour graphics layer, but they also expose higher-level visual treatments for emphasis and polish.

(gfx-sprite-glow player 4 1.8 255 180 90)
(gfx-sprite-outline player 2 255 255 255)
(gfx-sprite-shadow player 3 3 0 0 0 160)
(gfx-sprite-tint player 0.5 120 200 255)
(gfx-sprite-flash player 8 255 255 255)
  • gfx-sprite-glow adds a soft halo around an instance.
  • gfx-sprite-outline helps sprites read against noisy backgrounds.
  • gfx-sprite-shadow gives depth and separation.
  • gfx-sprite-tint and gfx-sprite-flash are useful for power-ups, damage, and hit feedback.
  • gfx-sprite-fx-off removes active sprite-side effects.
