Audio and Music

MacScheme exposes two audio layers to Scheme: sound-* for generated effects and music-* for ABC-based music playback and rendering. The fastest way to get results is to generate or load an asset once, then trigger playback explicitly when you need it.

Quick start

(audio-init)

(define coin (sound-coin 1.1 0.10))
(sound-play coin)

(define theme
  (abc
    "X:1"
    "T:Menu Theme"
    "M:4/4"
    "L:1/8"
    "Q:1/4=144"
    "K:C"
    "C2 E2 G2 c2 | b2 a2 g2 e2 |"))

(music-play theme)
  • audio-init is optional, but it gives deterministic startup.
  • sound-* constructors return sound ids. Call sound-play to actually hear them.
  • music-play accepts ABC text directly for quick experiments.
  • audio-stop-all stops both sound effects and music.

Sound effects

Use the built-in generators for arcade-style effects, UI clicks, pickups, zaps, and explosions.

(define laser (sound-shoot 0.8 0.12))
(sound-play laser 0.9 -0.2)

(define hit (sound-hurt 0.7 0.15))
(sound-play hit)
  • Built-ins include sound-coin, sound-jump, sound-shoot, sound-explode, sound-click, and more.
  • Use sound-stop-one, sound-free, and sound-export-wav when you need more control.

Custom synthesis

When you want a more specific tone, build it directly with waveform, filter, or FM parameters.

(define tone (sound-tone 440 0.25 'square))
(define note (sound-note 64 0.4 'saw 0.01 0.08 0.5 0.12))
(define filtered
  (sound-filter-tone 220 0.5 'saw 'lowpass 1200 0.8))
  • Waveforms: 'sine, 'square, 'saw, 'triangle, 'noise, 'pulse.
  • Filters: 'none, 'lowpass, 'highpass, 'bandpass.

ABC music in one minute

MacScheme’s music layer uses ABC notation, a compact text format for melodies, rhythm, and metadata. For quick sketches, you usually only need a handful of fields:

  • X: reference number
  • T: title
  • M: meter, such as 4/4
  • L: default note length
  • Q: tempo
  • K: key signature

Minimal ABC example

(define phrase
  (abc
    "X:1"
    "T:Phrase"
    "M:4/4"
    "L:1/8"
    "Q:1/4=120"
    "K:C"
    "C2 D2 E2 G2 | A2 G2 E2 D2 |"))

(music-play phrase 0.8)

Uppercase letters are notes in the middle register. Numbers after a note extend its duration. Bars are separated with |.

ABC player workflow

  • Use music-play when you want to audition a phrase or loop immediately.
  • Use music-load when you want to compile once and replay by id many times.
  • Use music-render when you want ABC-authored material to become a reusable sound asset.

Load, replay, and export

(define theme-id (music-load theme))
(music-play-id theme-id)

(define sting (music-render theme))
(sound-play sting)

(music-export-midi theme-id "theme.mid")
(music-render-wav theme "theme.wav")
  • music-pause, music-resume, music-stop, and music-volume control playback.
  • music-title, music-tempo, and related queries let you inspect loaded music data.
